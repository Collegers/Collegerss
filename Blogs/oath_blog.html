Narendra Modi to Take Oath for Third Term as Prime Minister

Narendra Modi, the Prime Minister-designate of India, will be taking his oath for a historic third consecutive term on June 9, Sunday. The ceremony will mark a significant milestone, making Modi the first person since Jawaharlal Nehru to be elected as Prime Minister for three consecutive terms. The oath-taking ceremony will begin at 7:15 PM IST and will be administered by President Draupadi Murmu at Rashtrapati Bhavan.

NDA's Dominant Performance

The NDA, led by the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), has secured an absolute majority in the Lok Sabha, winning a total of 292 seats. The BJP itself has emerged as the single largest party, claiming 240 seats out of the 543 that were contested. This victory underscores the enduring appeal of the BJP and its leadership under Prime Minister Narendra Modi, who has been a central figure in the party's strategy and outreach efforts.

Key Attendees and Dignitaries

The event will witness the attendance of several national and international dignitaries. Among the prominent figures present will be Defence Minister Rajnath Singh, Union Minister of State for Defence Ajay Bhatt, Chief of Defence Staff General Anil Chauhan, and Chief of Army Staff General Manoj Pande. Additionally, several leaders from neighboring countries, including the President of Sri Lanka, the President of Maldives, the Prime Minister of Bangladesh, and other dignitaries from Nepal, Bhutan, and Seychelles, have confirmed their participation.

Security Measures

In light of the high-profile nature of the event, security has been significantly heightened across the capital. The Rashtrapati Bhavan will be under a stringent three-tier security system until June 11. The Delhi-NCT area has been declared a no-fly zone to ensure the safety of the participants and to prevent any security breaches involving drones or other aerial devices. Approximately 2,500 police and paramilitary personnel have been deployed to maintain law and order.

Council of Ministers

Alongside Narendra Modi, members of his council of ministers will also take their oaths today. Media reports suggest that while the entire council may not be sworn in today, approximately thirty ministers are expected to take their oaths. The new council is anticipated to include 78 to 81 leaders, reflecting a greater reliance on coalition partners compared to previous terms.

International Presence and Invitations

The ceremony is also a demonstration of India’s commitment to its 'Neighbourhood First' policy. Dignitaries from the Indian Ocean region and neighboring countries have been specially invited. The confirmed attendees include President Ranil Wickremesinghe of Sri Lanka, President Mohamed Muizzu of the Maldives, Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina of Bangladesh, and other leaders from Bhutan, Nepal, and Mauritius.

Conclusion

The 2024 Lok Sabha elections have reinforced the vibrancy of Indian democracy, highlighting the importance of coalition politics and the dynamic nature of voter preferences. While the NDA celebrates its victory, the India Alliance's performance is a testament to the evolving political narrative in India. As the new government takes shape, the coming years will be critical in determining the direction of the nation and the efficacy of its democratic institutions.

Cabinet Changes and Speculations

Speculations about the new cabinet have been rife, with reports indicating significant changes. Smriti Irani and Anurag Thakur are reportedly not on the new cabinet ministers' list. On the other hand, S Jaishankar is expected to continue as the Minister for External Affairs. Additionally, leaders like Santanu Thakur from Bengal and BJP President JP Nadda are likely to be sworn in today.

Ceremony Details

The oath-taking ceremony will be held at Rashtrapati Bhavan, with President Draupadi Murmu administering the oath of office and secrecy. The event is expected to start at 7:15 PM IST and will be attended by various political leaders, international dignitaries, and around 250 workers who were involved in the construction of the new Parliament building.

Conclusion

As Narendra Modi prepares to take the oath for his third consecutive term, the event signifies a momentous occasion in Indian political history. With an array of national and international leaders in attendance and robust security measures in place, the ceremony promises to be a significant event reflecting India’s democratic strength and its strategic international relationships.
