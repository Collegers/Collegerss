iOS 18: Making the iPhone More Personal, Capable, and Intelligent Than Ever

Apple has once again redefined the smartphone experience with the preview of iOS 18. This major release introduces groundbreaking customization options, a comprehensive redesign of the Photos app, powerful new connectivity features, and the all-new Apple Intelligence system. These enhancements promise to make the iPhone more personal, capable, and intelligent than ever before.

Unparalleled Customization

With iOS 18, Apple has introduced new ways for users to personalize their iPhones. Users can now arrange apps and widgets in any open space on the Home Screen, including placing them above the dock for quick access or perfectly framing a wallpaper. App icons and widgets can adopt a dark or tinted effect and can be resized to suit individual preferences.

For the first time, users can also customize the controls at the bottom of the Lock Screen. This includes choosing from options available in the controls gallery or removing them entirely. The new Action button on the iPhone 15 Pro and iPhone 15 Pro Max allows users to quickly invoke controls from the gallery, enhancing accessibility.

The Control Center has been redesigned to provide easier access to frequently used controls. It now offers new levels of customization and flexibility, with quick access to groups such as media playback, Home controls, and connectivity, and the ability to easily swipe between these groups.

Photos: A Unified and Dynamic Experience

The Photos app has undergone its biggest-ever redesign in iOS 18. The app now features a simplified, single view with a familiar grid layout. New collections allow users to browse by themes without the need to organize content into albums. Favorite collections can be pinned for easy access, and a new carousel view presents daily highlights featuring favorite people, pets, places, and more.

Autoplaying content throughout the app brings photo libraries to life, allowing users to relive past moments while browsing. This dynamic new design makes it easier than ever to find and enjoy cherished memories.

Memories and Clean Up Tools

The new Memories feature in Photos lets users create the stories they want to see just by typing a description. Apple Intelligence will pick out the best photos and videos based on the description, craft a storyline with chapters based on themes identified from the photos, and arrange them into a movie with its own narrative arc. Additionally, a new Clean Up tool can identify and remove distracting objects in the background of a photo without accidentally altering the subject.

Big Updates to Safari

Safari, the world’s fastest browser, now offers an even easier way to discover information on the web with Highlights and a redesigned Reader experience. Using machine learning, Safari can surface key information about a webpage. Users can review a summary to get the gist of an article, see the location of a restaurant, hotel, or landmark, or listen to an artist’s track right from an article about the song or album. The redesigned Reader offers more ways to enjoy articles without distraction, including a summary and table of contents for longer articles.

Enhancements to Mail

Later this year, Mail will introduce new ways for users to manage their inbox and stay up to date. On-device categorization organizes and sorts incoming email into Primary for personal and time-sensitive emails, Transactions for confirmations and receipts, Updates for news and social notifications, and Promotions for marketing emails and coupons. Mail also features a new digest view that pulls together all of the relevant emails from a business, allowing users to quickly scan for what’s important in the moment.

Enhanced Connectivity and Privacy Features

iOS 18 introduces Messages over satellite, allowing users to communicate via the Messages app when cellular or Wi-Fi connections are unavailable. This feature builds on Apple's existing satellite capabilities and ensures users can stay connected in remote areas.

Privacy remains a core focus for Apple, and iOS 18 introduces new tools to manage app visibility, contact sharing, and accessory connections. Locked and hidden apps ensure that sensitive information remains private, offering peace of mind to users.

Introducing the Passwords App

Building on the foundation of Keychain, Apple has introduced the new Passwords app. This app makes it easy for users to access their passwords, passkeys, Wi-Fi passwords, and verification codes. This centralization of security information enhances convenience while maintaining Apple's high standards for privacy and security.

Apple Intelligence: Transforming the iPhone Experience

The highlight of iOS 18 is the introduction of Apple Intelligence, a personal intelligence system that combines generative models with personal context to deliver highly relevant and useful insights. Deeply integrated into iOS 18, iPadOS 18, and macOS Sequoia, Apple Intelligence leverages the power of Apple silicon to understand and create language and images, perform actions across apps, and simplify everyday tasks.

With privacy built from the ground up, Apple Intelligence offers new system-wide Writing Tools that enable users to rewrite, proofread, and summarize text across various apps, including Mail, Notes, Pages, and third-party applications.

More Features and Enhancements

Apple Maps and Game Mode

In Apple Maps, users can browse thousands of hikes across national parks in the United States and easily create custom walking routes, which can be accessed offline. Maps users can also save their favorite hikes, custom routes, and locations to an all-new Places Library and add personal notes.

Game Mode enhances the gaming experience with more consistent frame rates and makes wireless accessories like AirPods and game controllers more responsive.

Apple Pay and SharePlay

Users get new ways to pay with Apple Pay, including the ability to redeem rewards and access installments from eligible credit or debit cards. With Tap to Cash, users can send and receive Apple Cash by simply holding two iPhone devices together. Tickets in Apple Wallet now offer a richer experience with key event information, recommended Apple Music playlists, and more.

SharePlay with Apple Music allows users to share control of music playing from HomePod, Apple TV, or any Bluetooth-enabled speaker, making listening together more fun and engaging.

AirPods and Notes

The AirPods experience gets more personal and convenient with Siri Interactions, allowing AirPods Pro (2nd generation) users to respond to Siri announcements with head gestures. Voice Isolation ensures clearer call quality, and updates provide the best wireless audio latency for mobile gaming, along with Personalized Spatial Audio for immersive gameplay.

In the Notes app, formulas and equations are solved instantly with Math Notes, and new collapsible sections and highlighting make it easier to emphasize important content.

Journal and Calendar

In Journal, an all-new insights view helps users track journaling goals, with the ability to search and sort entries. Time spent journaling can be saved as mindful minutes in the Health app, and users can log their state of mind directly in Journal. A Journal widget allows quick entry from the Home Screen or Lock Screen, and audio recordings are automatically transcribed.

Calendar now shows both events and tasks from Reminders, allowing users to create, edit, and complete reminders directly from Calendar. The updated month view provides an overview of events and tasks.

Health and Emergency Features

The Health app has a redesigned Medical ID for easier access to vital information in emergencies. It can also help users understand their data during pregnancy with personalized adjustments and recommendations.

Emergency SOS Live Video allows users to share live video or recorded media during an emergency call, providing dispatchers with crucial context for faster assistance.

Home App and Accessibility

The Home app introduces guest access for control of smart home accessories and hands-free unlock with home keys, leveraging Ultra Wideband technology. Updates to the Energy category help users make informed decisions about home electricity use.

Accessibility updates include Eye Tracking for navigating iPhone with just eyes, Music Haptics for experiencing music through the Taptic Engine, and Vocal Shortcuts for performing tasks with custom sounds.

With iOS 18, the iPhone becomes more personal, capable, and intelligent than ever, setting a new standard for what a smartphone can do.

Conclusions

iOS 18 is a monumental release that brings unprecedented levels of customization, a major redesign of the Photos app, enhanced connectivity options, and the revolutionary Apple Intelligence system. As Craig Federighi, Apple’s senior vice president of Software Engineering, said, "This release also marks the beginning of a tremendously exciting new era of personal intelligence with Apple Intelligence delivering intuitive, powerful, and instantly useful experiences that will transform the iPhone experience, all with privacy at the core. We can’t wait for users to experience it." With iOS 18, the iPhone becomes more personal, capable, and intelligent than ever, setting a new standard for what a smartphone can do.
